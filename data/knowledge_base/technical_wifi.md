# Wi-Fi Troubleshooting Guide

**Guide version:** 1.0

Use this guide when the service works over a wired connection or on other networks but is slow, unstable, or unavailable over the customer's Wi-Fi.

## Safe troubleshooting sequence

1. Confirm whether all devices or only one device is affected.
2. Confirm the router has power and ask the customer to move within 3 metres for a test.
3. Turn Wi-Fi off and on for the affected device, then reconnect to the correct network.
4. Restart the router by disconnecting power for 30 seconds, reconnecting it, and waiting up to 5 minutes.
5. Test both the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz network when available. Use 2.4 GHz for longer range and 5 GHz for higher speed at short range.
6. Temporarily pause large downloads, VPNs, or other high-bandwidth devices and test again.

Never ask a customer to factory-reset the router unless a human support specialist authorizes it. If the internet/status light remains red, follow the Router Troubleshooting Guide. If multiple nearby customers are affected, follow the Network Outage Policy.

Create a technical ticket when these steps fail, noting affected devices, light status, wired-test result, and completed steps.
