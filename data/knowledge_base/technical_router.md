# Router Troubleshooting Guide

**Guide version:** 1.0

## Light checks

- No power light: verify the power cable, wall outlet, and power switch.
- Red or amber internet light: check the service cable, then perform one power restart.
- Normal internet light but no Wi-Fi light: enable Wi-Fi using the router button or supported app.
- Repeatedly flashing or rebooting lights: disconnect nonessential cables and create a hardware ticket if the loop continues.

## Restart procedure

Disconnect router power for 30 seconds. Reconnect it and allow up to 5 minutes for service to return. Do not repeat the restart more than twice in one support session because repeated restarts can obscure line diagnostics.

## Escalation

Check for a known regional outage before arranging replacement. Create a technical ticket if the red internet light continues after cable checks and one restart, if the router will not power on with a working outlet, or if physical damage or overheating is reported. Advise the customer to unplug equipment that smells burnt, sparks, or is unusually hot and escalate immediately as a safety issue.

Do not request router administrator passwords or instruct an unapproved factory reset.
